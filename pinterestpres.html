Overview

Breaking into a completely different industry can be difficult, especially if you don't have the traditional background for it. As such, I was very excited when Pinterest opened applications for their 2024 Product Designer Apprenticeship Program for candidates from non-traditional backgrounds. A part of the application was to make a 3-5 slidedeck describing yourself and showcasing the project that best represents you as a Product Designer, the perfect opportunity for me to flex my presentation muscle and showcase my design skills.

Process

Given this is a slidedeck for Pinterest, I wanted to make my slides Pinterest-themed by incorporating recognizable elements of Pinterest.

Formatting

So, should my slides evoke Pinterest's web or mobile UI? Given the overall dimensions of a slide, thinking about which would allow me to relay information more effectively, and considering how the hiring team was to likely view the slides, I opted for the web interface and sought inspiration from Pinterest's desktop web browser UI.
  • navigation bar
  • iconic Pinterest feed: feed of recommended Pins/posts/cards based on user activity and engagement

Content

Next was deciding what information to put on my slides. The guidelines were 1) showcase a project that best represents you as a project designer, and 2) include a page about yourself that outlines your background, experience, and design philosophy. And given that I only had 4-5 slides, I knew I needed to be intentional with what to include on each slide while making sure that everything was legible and easy to understand with even just a glance.

Good start, let's try again → version 2

Reflection

Overall, I really like the slideshow that I made and believe it to be a creative and accurate demonstration of who I am as a designer as well as person. Though the apprenticeship program didn't work out, it was a great opportunity still and gave me the chance to showcase and hone my skills. As a result, I'm a more effective presenter and writer with another experience under my belt :)!
